Frequently Asked Questions UTADA

What is HACKATHON 101?

Hackathon 101 is the Hakathon tailored specifically for lower-year students’ skill level. In this event, programmers and others involved in software development, including graphic designers, statisticians, and project managers, collaborate intensively for 24 hours to write a program.

Can I participate if I have no coding experience at all?

This event does not require its participants to have extensive programming knowledge. However, if you have no coding experience, we do not recommand you to participate. We are still aiming for low-year students' skill level.

What should I prepare before attending?

You do not need to prepare anything before attending. But after you register, make sure you attend our workshops. Two workshops will be held with industry professionals and university representatives, giving tutorials on development tools needed.

Can I attend this Hakathon alone?

At the end of each workshop, there will be a 1-hour social time. During this time you can find people to team up with. If you cannot find a team before the hakathon starts, don't worry, we will help you to pair up.

How much does it cost to attend Hackathon 101?

Admission to Hakathon 101 is completely free. We also provide food and accommodation for the entire event and help out as much as we can!

Can I attend if I am a upper year student?

The goal of this hackathon is to help lower-year students to be introduced to, and be prepared for, topics that are seen in higher-level hackathons that they would not otherwise attend, without the fear of feeling unwelcomed.

What kind of workshops, talks, and activities will there be?

Workshops and talks will be Intro to Web Development and Team Building. We’ve also had introductory workshops to various programming tools such as APIs, databases and platforms. Activities like VR demos, meetups happen throughout the weekend.

Can I start working on my hack before the event?

No. In the interest of fairness, students should not be working on their projects before Hackathon 101 begins and we do not allow participants to work on pre-existing projects.
